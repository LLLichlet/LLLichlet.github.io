1.5 Dimension

Definition 1.5.1.

Let V be a vector space over a field F. A subspace chain in V is a finite sequence of subspaces

{0}=V0⊊V1⊊⋯⊊Vk⊆V.
Definition 1.5.2.

For a subspace U⊆V, the dimension of U is

dimU=sup{k∣{0}=V0⊊⋯⊊Vk=U⁢ is a subspace chain}.

If the supremum does not exist, dimU=∞.

Proposition 1.5.3.

For any subspace U⊆V,

dimU+dim(V/U)=dimV.

(Here ∞+n=n+∞=∞+∞=∞.)

Proof.

Let π:V→V/U be the quotient map.

dimV≤dimU+dim(V/U). Take any subspace chain {0}=V0⊊⋯⊊Vk=V in V. For each i, either Vi−1∩U⊊Vi∩U, or π⁢(Vi−1)⊊π⁢(Vi) (or both): if neither were strict, then Vi−1∩U=Vi∩U and Vi−1+U=Vi+U; picking x∈Vi∖Vi−1 and writing x=y+u with y∈Vi−1, u∈U (from the second equality) gives u=x−y∈Vi∩U=Vi−1∩U⊆Vi−1, so x=y+u∈Vi−1, contradiction.

Thus the k steps are split into those where the intersection with U strictly grows, and those where the image in V/U strictly grows. After removing redundancies, the intersections form a chain in U, the images a chain in V/U, so k≤dimU+dim(V/U). Taking the supremum over all chains in V yields the inequality.

dimV≥dimU+dim(V/U). Take chains {0}=U0⊊⋯⊊Ua=U in U and {0}=W0⊊⋯⊊Wb=V/U in V/U. Let Vj=π−1⁢(Wj); then U=V0⊊⋯⊊Vb=V. Concatenating,

{0}=U0⊊⋯⊊Ua=U=V0⊊⋯⊊Vb=V

is a chain in V with a+b strict inclusions. Thus dimV≥a+b for any such a, b; taking suprema gives dimV≥dimU+dim(V/U). ∎

Proposition 1.5.4.

For any linear map f:V→W,

dim(ker⁡f)+dim(im⁡f)=dimV.
Proof.

By Proposition 1.5.3 with U=ker⁡f, dim(ker⁡f)+dim(V/ker⁡f)=dimV. By Proposition 1.4.5, V/ker⁡f≅im⁡f via an isomorphism Φ. Applying Φ (resp. Φ−1) to each term of a chain in V/ker⁡f (resp. im⁡f) gives a chain of equal length in im⁡f (resp. V/ker⁡f); hence dim(V/ker⁡f)=dim(im⁡f). Substituting yields the result. ∎
