1.6 Isomorphism Theorems

Proposition 1.6.1.

Let U,W⊆V be subspaces. Then

(U+W)/W≅U/(U∩W).
Proof.

Define f:U→(U+W)/W by f⁢(u)=u+W.

ker⁡f=U∩W: f⁢(u)=0 in (U+W)/W iff u∈W, and u∈U by definition.

im⁡f=(U+W)/W: every element of (U+W)/W has the form u+w+W=u+W=f⁢(u) for some u∈U, w∈W.

By Proposition 1.4.5, U/ker⁡f≅im⁡f, i.e. U/(U∩W)≅(U+W)/W. ∎

Proposition 1.6.2.

Let U⊆W⊆V be subspaces. Then

(V/U)/(W/U)≅V/W.
Proof.

Define f:V/U→V/W by f⁢(v+U)=v+W. This is well-defined: if v+U=v′+U, then v−v′∈U⊆W, so v+W=v′+W.

ker⁡f=W/U: f⁢(v+U)=0+W iff v∈W, i.e. v+U∈W/U.

im⁡f=V/W: every coset v+W is f⁢(v+U).

By Proposition 1.4.5, (V/U)/ker⁡f≅im⁡f, i.e. (V/U)/(W/U)≅V/W. ∎

Proposition 1.6.3.

For any subspaces U,W⊆V,

dim(U+W)+dim(U∩W)=dimU+dimW.
Proof.

From Proposition 1.6.1, (U+W)/W≅U/(U∩W). Isomorphisms preserve dimension (applying the isomorphism to each term of a chain gives a chain of equal length), so dim((U+W)/W)=dim(U/(U∩W)).

By Proposition 1.5.3, dim(U+W)=dimW+dim((U+W)/W), and dimU=dim(U∩W)+dim(U/(U∩W)).

Subtracting gives dim(U+W)−dimW=dimU−dim(U∩W), i.e. dim(U+W)+dim(U∩W)=dimU+dimW. ∎
