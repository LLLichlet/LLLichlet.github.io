1.4 Quotient Space

Definition 1.4.1.

Let V be a vector space over a field F and U⊆V a subspace. For v∈V, the coset of v modulo U is

v+U={v+u∣u∈U}.

The quotient space V/U is the set of all cosets

V/U={v+U∣v∈V},

with addition and scalar multiplication defined by

(v+U)+(w+U)=(v+w)+U,λ⁢(v+U)=λ⁢v+U,

for v,w∈V and λ∈F.

Definition 1.4.2.

The quotient map π:V→V/U is defined by

π⁢(v)=v+U.
Proposition 1.4.3.

π is a linear map, ker⁡π=U, and im⁡π=V/U.

Proof.

Linearity: π⁢(v+w)=(v+w)+U=(v+U)+(w+U)=π⁢(v)+π⁢(w), and π⁢(λ⁢v)=λ⁢v+U=λ⁢(v+U)=λ⁢π⁢(v). Kernel: π⁢(v)=0+U iff v+U=U iff v∈U. Image: every coset v+U is π⁢(v). ∎

Proposition 1.4.4.

V/U is a vector space over F.

Proof.

The operations are well-defined: if v+U=v′+U and w+U=w′+U, then v−v′,w−w′∈U, so (v+w)−(v′+w′)=(v−v′)+(w−w′)∈U, hence (v+w)+U=(v′+w′)+U; similarly, λ⁢v−λ⁢v′=λ⁢(v−v′)∈U, so λ⁢v+U=λ⁢v′+U.

The zero vector is 0+U=U. The additive inverse of v+U is (−v)+U. The eight vector space axioms follow directly from the corresponding axioms in V. ∎
