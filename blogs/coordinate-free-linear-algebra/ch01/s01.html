Definition

Let $F$ be a field. A vector space over $F$ is a set $V$ equipped with two operations

$$ \begin{aligned} + &\colon V \times V \to V, \quad (u, v) \mapsto u + v, \\ \cdot &\colon F \times V \to V, \quad (\lambda, v) \mapsto \lambda v, \end{aligned} $$

satisfying the following axioms:

  1. $(V, +)$ is an abelian group. That is, for all $u, v, w \in V$,
    • $(u + v) + w = u + (v + w)$,
    • there exists $0 \in V$ such that $v + 0 = v$,
    • for each $v \in V$ there exists $-v \in V$ such that $v + (-v) = 0$,
    • $u + v = v + u$.
  2. Scalar multiplication is compatible with field operations: for all $\lambda, \mu \in F$ and $u, v \in V$,
    • $\lambda(u + v) = \lambda u + \lambda v$,
    • $(\lambda + \mu)v = \lambda v + \mu v$,
    • $(\lambda\mu)v = \lambda(\mu v)$,
    • $1v = v$, where $1$ is the multiplicative identity of $F$.

Elements of $V$ are called vectors; elements of $F$ are called scalars.
