2.1 Definition

Definition 2.1.1.

Let V be a vector space over a field F. A linear functional on V is a linear map φ:V→F. The dual space of V, denoted V∗, is the set of all linear functionals on V:

V∗=Hom⁡(V,F)={φ:V→F∣φ⁢ is linear}.
Proposition 2.1.2.

V∗ is a vector space over F with operations defined pointwise:

(φ+ψ)⁢(v)=φ⁢(v)+ψ⁢(v),(λ⁢φ)⁢(v)=λ⋅φ⁢(v),

for all φ,ψ∈V∗, λ∈F, and v∈V.

Proof.

V∗⊆FV, and the pointwise operations are exactly those of the function space FV. We use the subspace criteria, where the zero functional v↦0 is linear; the sum of two linear maps is linear; a scalar multiple of a linear map is linear. Hence V∗ is a subspace of FV, and therefore a vector space. ∎

Definition 2.1.3.

For v∈V and φ∈V∗, we write

⟨φ,v⟩=φ⁢(v).

The map ⟨⋅,⋅⟩:V∗×V→F is called the natural pairing between V∗ and V.

Proposition 2.1.4.

The natural pairing is bilinear: for all v,w∈V, φ,ψ∈V∗, and λ∈F,

⟨φ+ψ,v⟩ =⟨φ,v⟩+⟨ψ,v⟩,
⟨λ⁢φ,v⟩ =λ⁢⟨φ,v⟩,
⟨φ,v+w⟩ =⟨φ,v⟩+⟨φ,w⟩,
⟨φ,λ⁢v⟩ =λ⁢⟨φ,v⟩.
Proof.

The first two follow from the definition of addition and scalar multiplication in V∗; the last two follow from the linearity of φ. ∎
